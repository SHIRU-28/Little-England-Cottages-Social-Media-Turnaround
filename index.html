01 — The Challenge

When I took over Little England Cottages' social media in April 2025, the numbers told a story — and it wasn't a flattering one.

Content output had dropped 61.5% over the previous period. Views were down 26%. Engagement was a flatline. The Facebook page had roughly 58,600 followers, but only 10 photos had gone out that month. Instagram was even quieter: 12 posts, 38 stories, and a reach of just 2,200.

The strange part? The product was excellent. Little England Cottages is the kind of place that sells itself the moment you walk in — English country architecture, underfloor heating, working fireplaces, tea plantation views, gated security. None of that was coming through in the content.

Posting was random. No rhythm, no real plan, no content pillars to speak of. And once you stop showing up consistently, the platforms quietly stop showing your stuff to anyone. That's the cycle the brand had fallen into. The potential was sitting there, untapped.

02 — The Strategy

First move: stop the bleeding. Then build something with actual structure behind it.

I moved the brand away from ad-hoc posting and built a content strategy around six clear pillars:

Pillar 01
Scenic & lifestyle experiences
Foggy mornings, fireplaces, garden walks — atmosphere that sells the stay before the booking.
Pillar 02
Property & investment highlights
For buyers, not just holidaymakers — positioning the cottages as a real-estate proposition.
Pillar 03
Guest stories
Real people, real stays — proof that the experience lands the way it should.
Pillar 04
Local culture
Nuwara Eliya, tea country, the surrounding experience — context that deepens the brand.
Pillar 05
Behind-the-scenes
Construction, design choices, the team — the craft behind the product, made visible.
Pillar 06
Offers & promotions
Tactical, not spammy — the right offer, in the right window, framed the right way.

Posting cadence got locked in. I identified peak engagement windows for the audience and built the schedule around them. Grid aesthetics became a real consideration — visual consistency, brand cohesion, the works.

Community management stopped being an afterthought. Every comment got a reply. Every DM got an answer. Sounds basic, but you'd be surprised how many brands skip this entirely.

03 — Execution

Strategy on paper is the easy part. Execution is where most plans fall apart.

Here's how I kept things moving month after month:

01
Monthly content calendars built around the six pillars, mapped to brand goals and seasonal moments.
02
Designer & video editor briefs — clear, specific, no vague "make it look nice" requests.
03
Weekly check-ins with the brand owner for approvals, alignment, and feedback.
04
Content creation oversight — photos, carousels, reels, stories, all routed through one consistent visual language.
05
Cross-platform publishing across Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok, each formatted properly (no lazy cross-posts).
06
Nothing flashy. Just consistent execution, week after week, for the full engagement period through March 2026.

05 — My Role

Hands-on, end-to-end. Not a consultant who drops a deck and leaves.

Social media strategy

Content pillars, posting cadence, platform-specific approach — the blueprint behind every post.

Content planning

Monthly calendars, campaign mapping, seasonal alignment — never a "what do we post today?" moment.

Creative direction

Briefing designers and video editors, reviewing drafts, holding the visual standard across every asset.

Community management

Replying to comments, handling DMs, managing the brand voice day to day — like a human, not a bot.

Client relations

Regular communication with the brand owner, approvals, feedback integration — true partnership, not vendorship.

Analytics & reporting

Performance tracking, monthly insights, strategy adjustments based on what the data was actually saying.

06 — Takeaways

Six months. One brand. A few lessons that stuck with me.

Lesson 01
Consistency beats intensity.
Posting every week for six months outperforms posting 30 things in one week and vanishing. The algorithm rewards rhythm, not bursts.
Lesson 02
Strategy isn't optional.
Random content doesn't grow a brand — it just fills space. Pillars, cadence, and intent matter more than people want to admit.
Lesson 03
Community is the moat.
Reply to comments. Answer DMs. People remember brands that act like humans. It's low effort, high return.
Lesson 04
Data tells you when to pivot.
Tracking metrics monthly meant I could spot what was working and double down — or cut what wasn't. No guessing, no gut calls.
Little England Cottages is in a completely different place now than where it was in April 2025. That's the work, and that's the result.
